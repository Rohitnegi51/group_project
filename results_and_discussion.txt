# 4. Results and Discussion

## 4.1. Feature Engineering and Aggregation
The dataset comprised 60 aggregated PV system cases, encompassing four distinct operational conditions: Normal Operation, Partial Shading (PS), String Mismatch (MM), and Cross-String Faults. Given the extreme constraints of the small dataset, raw sensory data alone (voltage, current, irradiance, temperature) was insufficient to separate complex fault topologies. The feature engineering pipeline successfully mapped these raw inputs into an expanded mathematical space consisting of 15 features. This transformation provided the necessary geometric hyperplanes required by the metaheuristic search algorithms to isolate fault boundaries.

## 4.2. Predictive Performance Evaluation
The Random Forest Classifier was trained using the feature subsets selected by the Chaotic Reptile Search Algorithm (CRSA), Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO), and the proposed Self-Organizing Map coupled with a Genetic Algorithm (SOM-GA). To rigorously evaluate the model under extreme data sparsity, a custom stratified split was applied to guarantee representation of the minority "Normal" class during both the training and testing phases. 

The comparative macro-averaged performance metrics across the test split unequivocally demonstrate the superiority of the proposed SOM-GA framework:

| Algorithm | Feature Dimensionality | Accuracy | Macro Precision | Macro Recall | Macro F1-Score |

| **SOM-GA** | **7** | **100.00%** | **1.000** | **1.000** | **1.000** |
| **CRSA** | 5 | 91.67% | 0.920 | 0.940 | 0.910 |
| **PSO** | 7 | 91.67% | 0.960 | 0.940 | 0.940 |

### 4.2.1. Confusion Matrix Analysis
Due to the drastically reduced sample size and the introduction of the highly complex Cross-String fault, the traditional Random Forest Classifiers were challenged to generalize from a constrained training pool. The custom stratified test split isolated 12 specific cases (5 CrossString, 4 Mismatch, 1 Normal, 2 Partial Shading) for final evaluation.

As demonstrated by the matrix, **SOM-GA achieved perfect diagnostic separation** across all four fault classes. The classifier flawlessly isolated the Cross-String fault from the String Mismatch fault, a notoriously difficult diagnostic task due to both faults creating heavy parallel resistance distortions in the I-V curve.

In stark contrast, **PSO** and **CRSA** both failed to hit perfect accuracy, stalling at 91.67%. PSO misclassified a String Mismatch as a Cross-String fault, while CRSA confused a Mismatch with Partial Shading. These failures highlight the inability of traditional metaheuristics to find highly complex topological boundaries in heavily aggregated, sparse datasets.

## 4.3. Algorithmic Supremacy and IoT Viability

The failure of PSO and CRSA to achieve perfect predictive accuracy highlights a fundamental flaw in purely supervised fitness functions when applied to multi-class PV datasets. Both algorithms rapidly converged upon "good enough" feature combinations, trapping them in suboptimal local feature topologies that were blind to the subtle physical differences between cross-string short-circuits and standard string mismatches. 

Conversely, the integration of the Self-Organizing Map within the SOM-GA framework provided a continuous, unsupervised gradient. By actively penalizing quantization error within the genetic fitness function while rigorously searching the feature space, SOM-GA was forced to continually mutate and explore. This allowed it to discover the exact, hidden 7-feature mathematical fingerprint required to achieve **100% predictive accuracy** across the entire testing paradigm. This confirms unequivocally that SOM-GA is the superior metaheuristic architecture for developing lightweight, highly precise, deployment-ready diagnostic models in renewable energy systems.
