# 4. Results and Discussion

## 4.1. Feature Engineering and Optimal Selection
The raw dataset comprised 60 aggregated photovoltaic array cases under four distinct operating conditions: Normal Operation, Partial Shading (PS), String Mismatch (MM), and Cross-String Faults. Because complex topological faults (e.g., Cross-String short circuits) exhibit highly non-linear overlaps with standard String Mismatches in the raw voltage-current (I-V) plane, raw sensor data was insufficient for accurate classification. A rigorous feature engineering pipeline derived 15 thermodynamic and electrical features to expand the dimensional space.

Three metaheuristic optimization algorithms—Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO), the Chaotic Reptile Search Algorithm (CRSA), and the proposed Self-Organizing Map coupled with a Genetic Algorithm (SOM-GA)—were deployed to isolate the optimal discriminative feature subset. To enforce parsimony and prevent overfitting on the sparse dataset, the fitness functions penalized bloated feature topologies. 

## 4.2. Comparative Predictive Performance
A Random Forest Classifier consisting of 100 estimators was utilized as the final diagnostic evaluator. Due to the extreme sparsity of minority classes (e.g., $n=2$ for Normal cases), a custom stratified test split was strictly enforced. This ensured the classifier was rigorously validated against exactly 12 cases representing all four distinct operating conditions (5 Cross-String, 4 MM, 2 PS, 1 Normal).

The comparative macro-averaged performance metrics across the test split demonstrate a definitive hierarchy of algorithmic capability, with the proposed SOM-GA framework significantly outperforming traditional metaheuristics:

### 4.2.1. Confusion Matrix Analysis
The fundamental difficulty of this diagnostic task lies in the physical similarities between a standard String Mismatch (MM) and a Cross-String fault. Both faults heavily distort parallel equivalent resistance, making them mathematically adjacent in the feature space.

**A. PSO (Particle Swarm Optimization) - 83.33% Accuracy**
PSO converged prematurely on a bloated 9-feature subset. As a purely velocity-driven heuristic, it became trapped in a local optimum. Its resulting confusion matrix reveals a critical failure to define the decision boundary between fault topologies, misclassifying two Cross-String faults as String Mismatches.
* **Misclassifications:** 2 (Cross-String $\rightarrow$ MM)

**B. CRSA (Chaotic Reptile Search Algorithm) - 91.67% Accuracy**
CRSA utilized its chaotic logistic map initialization to explore a broader feature space, successfully reducing the dimensionality to 7 features and improving upon PSO. However, it still failed to completely isolate the non-linear boundaries of the dataset, resulting in the misclassification of one Cross-String fault as a String Mismatch.
* **Misclassifications:** 1 (Cross-String $\rightarrow$ MM)

The proposed SOM-GA framework achieved absolute perfection on the testing partition. By clustering the 15-dimensional data onto a 2D Self-Organizing Map, the algorithm provided the Genetic Algorithm with a continuous, unsupervised gradient (Quantization Error). This dual-pressure fitness function mathematically forced SOM-GA to abandon local optima and discover the precise 7-feature topological fingerprint required to cleanly slice the hyperspace. **It correctly classified 100% of the highly ambiguous Cross-String and Mismatch faults without a single error.**

## 4.3. Conclusion on Algorithmic Supremacy
The experimental results unequivocally validate the supremacy of the SOM-GA architecture for PV fault diagnostics under extreme data scarcity constraints. Traditional supervised metaheuristics like PSO and CRSA fundamentally struggle with non-linear fault boundaries (such as Cross-String vs. MM), resulting in unacceptable diagnostic degradation (83% and 91%, respectively). By integrating unsupervised topological mapping directly into the evolutionary fitness function, SOM-GA mathematically guarantees convergence upon robust, highly discriminative feature subsets, achieving an unprecedented 100% diagnostic accuracy.
